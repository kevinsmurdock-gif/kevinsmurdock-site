My Story

Biography

Three Decades Building in Healthcare

Kevin S. Murdock grew up in Greenville, South Carolina. He attended Shannon Forest Christian School before enrolling at The Citadel, where he graduated in 1992 — Gold Star recipient, walk-on soccer player, and early graduate.

After a World Bank forestry project in Tunisia (1996–97), Kevin built one of AstraZeneca's top national pharmaceutical sales territories (2001–04), launching Nexium and Crestor across the South Carolina market.

In 2007, he founded Premier Medical Laboratory Services in Greenville. Over the next 15 years, it grew into the cornerstone of Diversified Medical Healthcare — a holding company operating five laboratories across five states (SC, NC, TX, UT, FL), along with CPT Medical manufacturing, Vessel Medical distribution, and OnGen software.

When COVID-19 hit in 2020, Premier Medical was the first private laboratory in South Carolina to receive FDA validation for COVID-19 testing. The company scaled to up to 300,000 tests per day with pooling, became a Yale SalivaDirect designated lab, processed over 2 million specimens, and was the first lab to detect the BA.2 and BA.4 Omicron variants in Florida. At its peak, the organization employed 450 people.

Today, Kevin continues building in Greenville SC — AI-powered platforms for independent medical reps, independent practices, voice automation for healthcare, and faith-based AI. Going first again.

The Network

Diversified Medical Healthcare — Six Labs. Six States.

The story most coverage misses is how many labs were operating under a single, publicly documented umbrella. Diversified Medical Healthcare (DMH) — founded in Greenville, SC in 2011 — was a holding company for six independently operated, separately licensed clinical laboratories across South Carolina, North Carolina, Texas, Utah, and Florida. Each lab held its own CLIA certification, its own licensed lab director, and operated under its own state regulatory framework.

At peak, the network employed 450 people and served customers in all 50 US states. In February 2022, DMH announced a $51 million expansion — 185 new SC jobs and 136,000 sq ft of new space at Garlington North — covered by PRNewswire, SCBio, and published on the South Carolina Governor's official .gov website. The announcement was made at SCBIO 2022, the state's Life Sciences Conference. The SC Secretary of Commerce endorsed it on the record. Kevin's quote that day: “Our mission is to positively impact patient lives with solutions that drive the healthcare industry forward.” (PRNewswire, Feb 23, 2022.)

Beyond the six clinical labs, DMH also included CPT Medical (manufactured 6M+ COVID test kits and custom surgical trays), Vessel Medical (distribution), OnGen (medical data management software), and product lines including RapidRona, MyHealthPro, and ConfirmD. This was not a single-lab operation — it was one of the largest publicly documented diagnostic networks based in South Carolina.
